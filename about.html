The Foundation

Who we are

DR Foundation, known as DRF, is a Section 8 non-profit based in New Delhi. We work at the intersection of education, sport, and health, with a focus on underprivileged youth and persons with disabilities across India.

The foundation grew out of Project Checkmate, which began in April 2016 as on-ground chess training at the National Association of the Blind in R.K. Puram, New Delhi. When the programme outgrew its informal structure, it formalised as a registered non-profit.

Today, DRF runs scholarship programmes, chess training initiatives, and health partnerships across India, aligned to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals 3 (Good Health & Well-being) and 4 (Quality Education).

Our Mission

“To empower the world through education, sport, health, and sustainability through directed projects as well as impact investing.”

Our Partners

The organisations behind our work

Every programme we run is built on a partnership. These are the NGOs, clinics, and organisations who have worked alongside us to make change possible.

National Association of the Blind (NAB)
R.K. Puram, New Delhi

Our primary and longest-standing partner. Project Checkmate’s on-ground chess training, scholarship ceremonies, dental camps, and infrastructure support (including a new refrigerator for home-science students) have all been delivered here.

Partner for NAB
Wheeling Happiness Foundation
New Delhi

Co-organised the Devajyoti Scholarships 2021 with DRF. Founded by Paralympic gold medalist Dr. Deepa Malik (Padma Shri, Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna) and her daughter Devika Malik, a fellow Queen’s Young Leader awardee.

Scholarship Collaboration
International Society of Human Welfare & Rehabilitation (ISHWAR)
New Delhi

The first NGO partner for Project Checkmate, working with approximately 20 underprivileged children in Phase 1 of the programme.

Education Collaboration
ACTit Network
Fiji

Delivered “Thoughts Aloud” mental health sessions for DRF students — an international collaboration supporting emotional wellbeing alongside academic progress.

Health Collaboration
Red is the New Green
Mumbai

Conducted telephonic menstrual hygiene education sessions for girls as part of DRF’s health initiatives — reaching students who may not otherwise have access to this information.

Health Collaboration
Kamal Dental Clinic
New Delhi

Dr. Ashu Gupta and Mr. Sarthak Gupta delivered in-person dental hygiene sessions at NAB, R.K. Puram. Mr. Sarthak Gupta also accompanied 10 students to the Annual Inter-NGO Sports Meet at Modern School Vasant Vihar.

Health Collaboration
Baala
New Delhi

Partnered with DRF to distribute reusable sanitary kits and educational booklets to women at Kushalta Vikas Kender, New Delhi — addressing menstrual health and hygiene at a grassroots level.

Health Collaboration
Ek Kadam Aur Foundation
India

Hosted an online lecture by Devanshi on the benefits of chess for their student community in March 2021, expanding awareness of Project Checkmate’s model across India.
