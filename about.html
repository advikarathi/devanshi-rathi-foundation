The Foundation

Who we are

The Devanshi Rathi Foundation — known as DR Foundation or DRF — is a Section 8 non-profit company incorporated in New Delhi on 18 June 2018. We work at the intersection of education, sport, and health, with a focus on underprivileged youth and persons with disabilities across India.

The foundation grew out of Project Checkmate, which Devanshi launched in April 2016 while in Class 11, running on-ground chess training at the National Association of the Blind in R.K. Puram, New Delhi. When the project outgrew its informal structure, she formalised it as a registered non-profit — and DRF was born.

Today, DRF runs scholarship programmes, chess training initiatives, and health partnerships across India, aligned to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals 3 (Good Health & Well-being) and 4 (Quality Education).

About the Founder

Devanshi Rathi

Devanshi Rathi was born in 1999 in New Delhi. She started playing chess at age eight at Modern School, Vasant Vihar, and was coached from around age nine by IM Vishal Sareen. Chess became more than a game — it became a lens through which she understood focus, strategy, and the cost of inequality in access to opportunity.

In 2016, at 16, she won a Bronze Medal at the Commonwealth Chess Championship Under-18 Girls category in Waskaduwa, Sri Lanka — and in the same year, launched Project Checkmate, her first attempt to use chess as a social tool. She started by teaching underprivileged children at the International Society of Human Welfare and Rehabilitation, then expanded to the National Association of the Blind, R.K. Puram.

She was selected as a Queen’s Young Leader Highly Commended Runner-Up in 2018, attending the University of Cambridge’s Institute of Continuing Education as part of the programme. That same year, she registered the Devanshi Rathi Foundation as a formal Section 8 non-profit.

She went on to study at the University of California, Berkeley (2018–2022), where she designed her own Interdisciplinary Studies Field major and served as Co-President of the UC Berkeley Chess Club alongside WIM Ashritha Eswaran — the first time the club had two female co-presidents. She graduated in 2022.

Devanshi has written for The Bridge, ChessBase India, DailyO, and the UC Berkeley SCET blog, and has been profiled by WeCapable, The Optimist Citizen, ScooNews, and the Women Economic Forum. She has spoken at the Indian Youth Forum Awards and the Women Economic Forum.
