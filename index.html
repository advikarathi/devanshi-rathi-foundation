What We Do

Our Programmes

Three interconnected pillars that give young people — regardless of ability, background, or means — a genuine shot at the future they deserve.

♟

Project Checkmate

Started in 2016, Project Checkmate uses chess to promote education, cognitive growth, and wellbeing — with a focus on visually impaired and underprivileged youth. We have produced two internationally FIDE-rated players and organised the North Zone Chess Championship for the Blind.

Explore the programme
◇

DRF Scholarships

Five editions since 2018 — including the Devajyoti and Shaktinaari Scholarships — awarding cash prizes and year-long mentorship to students with disabilities aged 15–30. One scholar went on to earn a full merit scholarship to Ashoka University.

Health Initiatives

Partnering with organisations across India and beyond — dental camps, menstrual hygiene education, mental health sessions, and the DRF Purposeful Living Series — because empowerment starts with a healthy body and mind.

Our Story

Started on a chessboard.
Built for a generation.

“Chess taught me that every move matters. The DR Foundation is built on that same belief — that every young person, regardless of circumstance, deserves a chance to make their move.”

Devanshi Rathi founded Project Checkmate in 2016 while still in Class 11 at Modern School, Vasant Vihar — running on-ground chess training at the National Association of the Blind, R.K. Puram. Two years later, she formalised it as the Devanshi Rathi Foundation, a Section 8 non-profit registered in New Delhi.

A Commonwealth Chess Championship bronze medalist, Queen’s Young Leader Highly Commended Runner-Up, and UC Berkeley graduate, Devanshi brings both lived experience and institutional credibility to the work.
