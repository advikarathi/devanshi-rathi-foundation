Chess Achievement

Producing internationally rated players

One of DRF’s most concrete measures of impact: students from underprivileged and visually impaired communities achieving recognition on the international chess stage.

2
International FIDE-Rated Players
Produced through Project Checkmate

Two students from Project Checkmate have achieved international FIDE ratings — a landmark outcome for a programme working primarily with visually impaired and underprivileged youth who had no prior access to competitive chess infrastructure.

The first, Ayush Prakash Jaiswal, earned his FIDE Rapid Rating after the Chessmine Rapid Tournament (September 2017 FIDE list). He later won a full merit scholarship to Ashoka University under DRF mentorship.

♟ FIDE Rapid Rating achieved 2017
2
National Championship Qualifiers
National “B” Chess Championship for the Blind, 2017

Two Project Checkmate students qualified for the National “B” Chess Championship for the Blind in 2017 — competing at the national level after just months of structured training through the programme.

DRF students also won at the 1st National Schools Chess Championship for the Blind in Shirdi, Maharashtra — and participated in the North Zone Chess Championship for the Blind, organised by DRF itself at NAB, R.K. Puram in October 2017.

🏆 National Championship Qualifiers 2017

Grandmaster Interactions

Project Checkmate students have participated in simultaneous exhibition sessions with top international chess masters and coaches — an opportunity that broadened their exposure to competitive chess.
