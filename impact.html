Scholar Stories

The people behind the numbers

These are some of the scholars and students whose journeys the DR Foundation has been a part of — from chess training to university scholarships.

Ayush Prakash Jaiswal DRF Scholar 2018 & 2019 — Ashoka University scholarship recipient

"Hi, I am Ayush, the winner of the D.R.F. scholarship of 2018 and 2019. This award gave me the motivation to work harder for my best future interests. While applying, we had to write an application about our accomplishments and the best ones were chosen. I received a cash prize along with a gift hamper. The award helped me pay the fees for various academic events and entrance exams. However, the most important was the guidance and mentorship, which was given to me throughout the whole year in fields of academics, sports, and extracurriculars. This helped me a lot. The scholarship is an excellent platform for the students to enhance their skills."

– Ayush Jaiswal '18 & '19 winner and a recipient of a full college scholarship at Ashoka University under DRF's mentorship

KB Khushi Bisht DRF Scholar 2018 winner

"I am very glad to be the winner of DRF scholarship 2018 and would like to express my gratitude to Devanshi mam because she gave me a chance to participate in this program. It was a wonderful learning experience as a winner for a period of one year as I received special guidance from Devanshi mam, both online and offline support that helped me perform better in my academics. I used my prize money to participate in various other competitions including chess tournaments, which helped me to gain experience and improve my skills in the game."

– Khushi Bisht '18 winner

Neeraj Sharma DRF Scholar 2019 — Grade 9, Bholpur, Rajasthan

"I am Neeraj Sharma, a student in grade 9, originally from Bholpur, Rajasthan. I play chess and cricket in my time apart from school. I participated in the second DRF Scholarships and was a winner this time around. This prize boosted my confidence and also gave me a lot of motivation to succeed further in my goals. Thanks to the scholarship, I am able to play in a lot more tournaments outside. Currently, I am working hard to get to the next level in chess by getting my FIDE rating."

– Neeraj Sharma '19 winner

Mrunali Pande Project Checkmate learner and chess player

"I learnt about DRF from my friends. I then followed the activities on social media to explore more about the foundation. I am amazed at the drive shown by the organisation towards hosting impactful events and sessions. I look forward to working with the foundation and growing as a chess player."

– Mrunali Pande

MP Muskan Patwari DRF Scholar 2020 winner

"I learnt and understood so many diverse aspects of life through the scholarship. The mentoring program helped me to connect with people whose success stories were highly inspirational. Overall, I'm grateful to DRF for selecting me as one of their scholars."

– Muskan Patwari '20 winner

SJ Shivangi Jethwani Virtual DRF volunteer

Chess Achievement

Producing internationally rated players

One of DRF’s most concrete measures of impact: students from underprivileged and visually impaired communities achieving recognition on the international chess stage.

2
International FIDE-Rated Players
Produced through Project Checkmate

Two students from Project Checkmate have achieved international FIDE ratings — a landmark outcome for a programme working primarily with visually impaired and underprivileged youth.

The first, Ayush Prakash Jaiswal, earned his FIDE Rapid Rating after the Chessmine Rapid Tournament (September 2017 FIDE list). He later won a full merit scholarship to Ashoka University under DRF mentorship.

♟ FIDE Rapid Rating achieved 2017
2
National Championship Qualifiers
National “B” Chess Championship for the Blind, 2017

Two Project Checkmate students qualified for the National “B” Chess Championship for the Blind in 2017 — competing at the national level after just months of structured training.

DRF students also won at the 1st National Schools Chess Championship for the Blind in Shirdi, and participated in the North Zone Championship organised by DRF at NAB in October 2017.

🏆 National Championship Qualifiers 2017

Grandmaster Interactions

Project Checkmate students have participated in simultaneous exhibition sessions with top international chess masters and coaches — an opportunity that broadened their exposure to competitive chess.
