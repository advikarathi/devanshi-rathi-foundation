Programme 01

Project Checkmate

Started April 2016 · Pan-India
Using chess to unlock education,
opportunity, and wellbeing

Project Checkmate is DRF’s flagship programme — using chess as a pedagogical tool to promote cognitive development, focus, and self-confidence in underprivileged and visually impaired youth. What began as a small training session at an NGO in New Delhi has grown into a pan-India programme producing internationally rated players.

Phases of the programme

Phase 1
On-Ground Training at NAB
April 2016 onwards

Launched at ISHWAR, then expanded to the National Association of the Blind (NAB), R.K. Puram. Students competed at state and national blind chess championships. Produced the first FIDE-rated blind chess player — Ayush Prakash Jaiswal.

Phase 2.0
Project Checkmate 2.0
June – August 2020

Specialised online programme for the top 15 visually impaired chess players in India. Featured guest speakers from sports psychology, nutrition, entrepreneurship, and physiotherapy, plus simultaneous chess exhibitions with Indian Grandmasters.

Phase 3.0
Project Checkmate 3.0
October – November 2020

Online boot camp focused on chess for women and girls, coached by a National Blind and Para Asian Chess medalist. Aimed at expanding access to competitive chess for women in India.

Special Programme
Queen’s Gambit Girls Chess Club
January – March 2021

Virtual club for girls to learn chess and attend personal development workshops. Three students were selected for ongoing mentoring of their chess careers following the programme.

Tournament
DRF Chess Tour for the Disabled
January – February 2021

Online tournament series for players with disabilities. Winners of each event received special Grandmaster sessions; overall winners received monetary prizes.

Landmark Event
North Zone Chess Championship for the Blind
October 2017 · NAB, New Delhi

Organised by DRF at NAB, R.K. Puram — the biggest blind chess championship of its kind held to that date in North India. A milestone moment for inclusive competitive chess.

Programme highlights

♟
First FIDE-Rated Blind Player

Ayush Prakash Jaiswal earned his FIDE Rapid Rating after the Chessmine Rapid Tournament (September 2017 FIDE list) — the first student from the programme to achieve an international rating.

♛
Grandmaster Interactions

Students have had simultaneous exhibition sessions with top international chess masters and instructors.

🏆
National Championship Qualifiers

Two students qualified for the National “B” Chess Championship for the Blind 2017; students also won at the 1st National Schools Chess Championship for the Blind, Shirdi.

🎓
Ashoka University Scholarship

Ayush Jaiswal, a Project Checkmate scholar, went on to win a full merit scholarship to Ashoka University — a landmark outcome for the programme.

Programme 02

DRF Scholarships

Since 2018, DRF has run five editions of its scholarship programme — awarding cash prizes and year-long mentorship to meritorious students, primarily from marginalised or disability backgrounds. Scholars range from 15 to 30 years old and come from across India.

Mentorship includes guidance on academics, sports, and extracurriculars; access to partner programmes; and speaker events and webinars. Each edition has a distinct theme, reflecting DRF’s evolving focus on inclusion, disability, and women’s empowerment.

Scholarship criteria & format
  • Age range: 15–30 years
  • Selection via essay or application on life, future goals, and chosen activity
  • Cash prize for winners
  • Year-long mentorship programme
  • Access to partner programmes, webinars, and speaker events
  • Focus on persons with disabilities and marginalised communities
Edition 1
Inaugural DRF Scholarships
July 2018 · NAB, New Delhi

The first edition of DRF’s scholarship programme, held at the National Association of the Blind, R.K. Puram. Students submitted essays on their lives and future goals.

Notable winner
Ayush Jaiswal
Later won a full scholarship to Ashoka University under DRF mentorship
Edition 2
Second DRF Scholarships

Notable winners

Grade 9, Rajasthan; chess & cricket player; working toward FIDE rating
Edition 3
Third DRF Scholarships
July 2020 · Virtual

First virtual edition, held during COVID-19. Open to persons with disabilities from all over India. Four prize winners plus participation rewards for all participants.

Edition 4
Devajyoti Scholarships

Co-organised with Wheeling Happiness Foundation (Dr. Deepa Malik). “Devajyoti” means Divine Light. Targeted persons with disabilities aged 15–30 across 6 categories.

DRF Virtual Scholars Group

Programme 03

Health Initiatives

○
Our Philosophy

“A healthy body rests in a healthy mind.” DRF views health as integral to its empowerment mission — not an add-on, but a foundation.

DRF’s health initiatives span mental health, menstrual hygiene, dental care, physical activity, and nutritional support — delivered through partnerships with NGOs and healthcare professionals across India and internationally.

ACTit Network · Fiji
Mental Health — “Thoughts Aloud”

Online mental health sessions for DRF students, delivered in partnership with the ACTit Network from Fiji — an international collaboration bringing global expertise to Indian youth.

Mental Health
Red is the New Green · Mumbai
Menstrual Hygiene Education

Telephonic education sessions for girls on menstrual health and hygiene, reaching students who may not have access to this information through formal channels.

Women’s Health
Kamal Dental Clinic · New Delhi
Dental Hygiene Camp at NAB

In-person dental hygiene sessions at NAB, R.K. Puram, delivered by Dr. Ashu Gupta and Mr. Sarthak Gupta, who also accompanied 10 students to the Annual Inter-NGO Sports Meet.

Dental & Physical
Baala · New Delhi
Sanitary Kit Distribution

Distribution of reusable sanitary kits and educational booklets to women at Kushalta Vikas Kender, New Delhi — addressing menstrual health at a grassroots community level.

Women’s Health
Ms. Jyoti Mishra · Yoga & Wellness
DRF Purposeful Living Series

Online coaching series empowering people with disabilities to lead more effectively. Episode 1 featured Ms. Jyoti Mishra covering mindfulness and purposeful daily living.

Mental Wellness
Infrastructure · NAB Delhi
Nutritional & Infrastructure Support

DRF distributed nutritional meals to students and installed a new refrigerator at NAB Delhi, enabling students with multiple disabilities to prepare for home-science education exams.
